Mambo Drone: Autonomous Line-Following and Control Systems Design

Summary

Created a low-level software architecture for the Mambo mini drone, integrating control systems, sensor fusion, and computer vision to enable precise autonomous line-following capabilities. The project involved implementing sophisticated control algorithms to achieve stable flight dynamics, allowing the drone to maintain balance and responsiveness during autonomous operations. Sensor fusion techniques were used to combine data from the Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) and camera systems, providing accurate localization and orientation information that was crucial for maintaining an optimal path. The computer vision component utilized advanced image processing techniques to detect and track line markings on the ground, ensuring the drone could accurately follow a pre-defined route. These techniques included filtering, edge detection, and feature extraction to handle varying line visibility and environmental conditions. This solo project showcased the successful orchestration of advanced features for autonomous navigation.
