Hexapod Kinematics Simulator

Summary

This project focuses on modeling and controlling the movement of a hexapod robot using forward and inverse kinematics. The hexapod, a six-legged robot, was designed to demonstrate efficient leg coordination and body stability for various types of movement, including roll, pitch, yaw, and translation. The project utilized homogeneous transformation matrices and kinematic computations to enable the hexapod's precise movements.The project was a collaborative effort, along with a team of 5, and was undertaken during a robotics course in Master's Degree.

My Contibutions

I computed the DH parameters for the hexapod robot legs, providing the foundational geometric descriptions and also formulated the forward kinematics to determine the end effector (foot) position of each robot leg based on joint angles, enabling accurate control over its movement. Developed the inverse kinematics for the robot legs, solving for the required joint angles for the legs to reach specific target points. This work involved handling multiple constraints, avoiding positions below the ground and maintaining balance during movement.
